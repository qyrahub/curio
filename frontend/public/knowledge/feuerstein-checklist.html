Cognitive functions checklist

A working reference for locating where thinking gets stuck — and naming what's already strong. Each row pairs a growth area (what a struggle looks like) with the cognitive function it corresponds to (what it looks like when it's working).

How to use this

Don't score a child on all of it at once. Bring one piece of real work or one real moment to mind, and tick only what you actually observed. Ticks on the left name something to build; ticks on the right name a strength to lean on. Both matter — and both change over time, which is exactly the point.
